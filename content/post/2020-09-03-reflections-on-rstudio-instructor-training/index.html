The what

The best place the start is to read about the RStudio Instructor Training and Certification Program here. The process boils down to three steps.

(1) Training Course

The first step is to sign up for the training course, which will likely be held over Zoom and chunked into 3-4 hour segments across 2-3 days. You can access the training materials here.

The training course is an interactive introduction to applying evidence-based teaching methods, such as learning personas, concept maps, and formative assessment (i.e.¬†using questions or exercises to figure out if learners are forming accurate mental models), to data science education. The course also covers how to design teaching materials that take into account cognitive load, multiple learning styles, and issues of inclusivity and student motivation in the classroom.

(2) Technical Exam

The tidyverse exam assesses technical skills in the Tidyverse by a series of live coding challenges related to data cleaning and wrangling, data visualization, string manipulation, functional programming, basic statistical modeling, and creating reproducible documents with R Markdown. As many others have suggested, a great way to prepare for this exam is to work through the exercises R for Data Science, particularly for topics that feel rusty to you, and review the community-contributed solutions.

(3) Teaching Exam

The first certification exam assesses pedagogical skills related to teaching data science with R and requires giving a demonstration lesson, creating formative assessments on unseen material (e.g.¬†multiple choice questions and fill-in-the-blank coding exercises), developing concept maps on data science topics, and giving feedback on example educational exercises based on pedagogical theory.
